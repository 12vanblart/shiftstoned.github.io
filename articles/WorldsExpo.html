Pictures at an Exhibition Hanging this year's Championship Power bases on the wall, and introducing the Eternal Power Calculator's new titling feature
We take a look at Power base visualizations for all 16 decks from the Eternal Tournament Series 2018 World Championship
Follow @shiftstoned


December 14, 2018 - Congratulations to all of the Eternal Card Game players who qualified for RNGEternal's Eternal Tournament Series 2018 World Championship — especially to Tobboo of the Eternal Titans for their first place finish, following a trail blazed by teammate camat0 who was last year's ETS World Champion.

To commemorate the conclusion of the 2018 series, we have assembled here a gallery of Power base visualizations for each of the Championship decks, from Skycrag to Chalice.

As you browse the collection, check out the Eternal Power Calculator's newest feature: You can now name and title your deck  when you create a unique link to share your list with the world. (Or keep it to yourself).



May you find inspiration for your own brews while exploring the Power behind these top decks. And be sure to add a title when creating your next masterpiece.

ETS 2018 World Championship Finishers

1st  P L A C E:

► Rakano Ramp by Tobboo

The inclusion of Granite Waystones ended up paying off for Tobboo, as 1/1 Grenadins played a significant role against Relic Weapon based strategies in the finals. Flame Blast (2) probability seems deceptively low after two draws, but realistically this spell will be played much later. A total of 38 Justice sources ensures that Amili, Cloudmarshal (5) will fly on time. And of course several cards put the “Ramp” into Rakano, including: Icaria, Valkyrie Captain (2) , Yushkov the Usurper (4) and Bulletshaper.

2nd  P L A C E:

► Worlds Deck FTJ Armory by Erik9099

Ten Crests allow Erik9099 to Scout frequently for the cards they need. Justice is quite strongly prioritized over the other two Factions to enable Throne Warden (5) and the decks six sweepers, including a full set of Harsh Rule) Meanwhile Privilege of Rank (2) is so valuable, it is included even in the absence of any discard effects.

3rd  P L A C E:

► Cheesegrater 250 by Kangbreath

It never hurts to have a Crownwatch Tactic or two in hand, and Kangbreath can afford to include Transmute sources in a deck that tops out at five Power for Tavrod, Auric Broker.

► Mystical Xenan by SecondBlue

The abilities of Worldbearer Behemoth and Waystone Infuser help to ensure that Power keeps flowing through Mystic Ascendant, the potent 6-drop Unit that gives this deck its name. In a two-faction Time deck there is very little downside to including a full set of Amber Waystones, as SecondBlue does here.





5th P L A C E:

► 8 Merchant Blitz by Almost

Diplomatic Seals make it easier to play early units with demanding Influence requirements, especially in a three-faction list like this one. A single Crownwatch Standard creates the potential for an additional combat trick to fuel Almost's twelve-plus Units with pump spell synergy.

► BBG SecondBlue's Combrei Mid by childroland

When you count Power draw spells and Power granting Units, this deck includes more Power sources than any other in the event. Eight Sigils are waiting to be pulled by Lingering Influence or the abilities of Icaria, Vaklyrie Captain and Telut, Queen's Hand. Marshal Ironthorn and Mystic Ascendant allow childroland to get maximum value from all that Power being drawn.

► ET Manu's FJS Zuberi by Mouche

A deck that foregoes Icaria, the Liberator can go a little lighter on the sources. This list prioritizes and instead for midrange threats such as Vicious Highwayman, Zuberi, Outlands Warrior and Vara, Venegance-Seeker. Still, there is enough to confidently play Rizahn, Greatbow Master on curve.

► I Couldn't Make Unitless Work So I'm Playing As Many Units As Possible by TinMan

As the title implies, the strategy for this deck involves units. But that does not mean giving up on Power sources. A full set of Initiate of the Sands makes it easier to reach the 8 Power necessary to gain maximum value from Xenan Obelisk. Trail Maker pulls triple duty as a unit as well as both a Power and Influence source. Meanwhile, a single Amber Acolyte does a convicing impersonation of Seek Power with a body.


R U N N E R S  U P:

► Blue Merchant Skycrag by Paradox

A deck with twelve 1-drops demands undepleted Power and versatile Influence from the opening hand, so a full set of Diplomatic Seals is a natural choice. Shugo Standard provides extra utility for Paradox's aggressive strategy, and is a safe bet in a deck that tops out at 3 Power (Ultimates and Spellcraft abilities notwithstanding).

► Chalice by camat0

Seek Power and Privilege of Rank help to increase camat0's chances of reaching after 4 draws for Shelterwing Rider. While it would seem this list also wants TT on Turn 4, the only cards with that Influence cost are specific answers in the Market (Sword of Unity, Lumen Reclaimer). Those will realistically be played much later in the game.

► ETS World Championship Rakano by ThePlatypusKing

Justice gets priority over Fire for early Units such as Unseen Commando and Valkyrie Enforcer. A single Cauldron Cookbook provides another example of how Markets allow players to include cards which their Power base might not otherwise support, since ThePlaytupusKing has control over when he draws the powerful Relic. And of course there is enough Influence to confidently play the mighty Rakano Plate on time.

► Feln Mid by TonyGeeeee

An even split between Primal and Shadow sources (including Vara's Favor 2S), which makes sense in a deck wants to play a fully enabled Champion of Cunning (5PS). Four Cobalt Waystones protect against hand disruption and other face-targeting effects. Wisdom of the Elders and/or Strategise provide a couple of addional draws before Turn 5, helping TonyGeeeee reach the influence requirements for Jotun Feast-Caller (5PPP) and enabling even more additional draws.

► Get the Bridge by SooNo

Twenty-six Power cards evenly split between Time and Justice are bolstered by an additional 12 secondary sources in this high voltage deck. Awakened Student derives extra value from Power played in the early game while Mystic Ascendant does the same on later on. Worldbearer Behemoth and Telut, Queen's hand provide assistance with their Power drawing abilities.

► Maiden's Rest by BruisedByGod

Shadow (Sx24) is highly prioritized over Fire (Fx17) in this Stonescar midrange list which aims to hit SSS on Turn 5 for Shadowlands Tyrant. Fire cards with more than a single F are relegated to the market, where BruisedByGod can draw them once the requirements have been met. A smattering of Standards create potential for surprising Overwhelm and Quickdraw attacks, which pairs nicely with the deck's Deadly units.

► Manus Ambitious Kerendon Midrange by lv13david

An identical list to the one brought by lv13david's teammate Mouche, who piloted it to a 5th place finish on Day Two of the tournament.

► Teamrankstar.com Tokens by Magikarp

Time is slightly favored over Fire to prioritize early units such as Initiate of the Sands and Teacher of Humility. A full set of Granite Waystones potential provides extra bodies for Magikarp's go-wide strategy.

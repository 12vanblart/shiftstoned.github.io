Danger: High Voltage More than half the cards in this tournament winning deck piloted by Mouche
are Power sources. "I heard Alessi is still free to grow" raises the question: When is
too much Power just enough?






 Power to those who seek it Eternal card game requries a minimum of 25 Power Cards in order for your deck to be valid. Decks that may be especially Power-hungry  will commonly include a few extras, sometimes up  to a total of 28.

At the Eternal Tournament Series recent S3W3 event, Mouche took first-place piloting a deck that includes no less than 32 Power Cards. Overall there is a grand total of 39 Power sources when you include Find the Way and Auralian Merchant.

That means over half of the cards in the deck are Power sources. For most lists, this would be a recipe for flooding. But as their first-place finish demonstrates, Mouche is making it work somehow.The first step: InnovationWith all that Power you might expect to see a lot of high-cost finishers such Sword of the Sky King or maybe even something like Novaquake Titan. But nope...the curve for Mouche's deck tops out at 4.

Hmm...ok. Maybe the deck has high Influence requirements then?

That's not it either. None of the cards require triple Influence, and the steepest Influence cost in the entire deck is  for Sword of Unity. 

If not greedy Power costs or Influence requirements then, what is all that Power for exacly?

After a closer look, it becomes clear that there are a specific combination of reasons why an overload of Power works for this deck in particular. 

Take this

On the most obvious level, Power ensures that impacful 1- and 2-drops can be played on time. Although Alessi Combrei Archmage,  Awakened Student and Teacher of Humility are all low-cost units in terms of Power, they each have Influence requirements that are a little trickier.

Unlike an Aggro deck that plays low-cost single Influence units such as Oni Ronin or Crownwatch Paladin, Mouche's deck needs to work a bit harder to meet  or . 

The pay-off is that each of these units can quickly run away with a game. Any one of them means a strong start. And a quick glance at the Power Odds table shows that Mouche has a very favorable chance of being able to play any one of them on curve. 




Mind over body

Speaking of Awakened Student, the 2-cost Combrei mystic can be very useful when it comes to converting your Power into a threat. The mechanic is called Empower for a reason.

With one or more of these guys in play, your Power cards essentially  become zero-cost spells that might as well read "Give your Awakened Students +1/+1."

It is also worth mentioning that (in addition to being a fifth pull for Crownwatch Press-gang), Wandering Wisp becomes a very powerful threat that quickly snowballs  after . This helps to explain why this deck includes a whopping 10 time sigils in the Power base.

Standard tactics

Perhaps most importanly, the Power base here takes full advantage of a new type Power card that was introduced with the Fall of Argenport. 

A ful contingent of Standards are included, and these synergize very well with Ahlessi, Combrei Archmage - another new card that has demonstrated her importance and spawned a range of deks bearing her name.
 


Power in the form of the Standards transmutes into efficient, low-cost Fast Spells. Crownwatch Tactic and Temple Tactic are useful on their own, but when they become  fuel for Ahlessi as well as all those Power sources don't seem so crazy after all.

Something similar could be said for  Find the Way. It's always nice to get 2x as many cards thanks to Echo, but when those cards are also growing your units you are getting twice the value.
 


Power Cards must be doing something more than being used to play cards in this deck. And indeed they are. They play an important role in enabling early plays of critical role players such as Awakened Student and (Most Likely to get nerfed candidate) Teacher of Humilty. But the power sources in this deck play other roles: fueling Awakened Student pumps, standards provide more spellls to activate Ahlessi, Find the Way also powers Ahlessi, Seek Power not needed because it is a two faction deck.
